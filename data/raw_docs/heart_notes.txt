The human heart is a muscular organ responsible for pumping blood throughout the body.
It consists of four chambers: two atria and two ventricles.
Oxygen-poor blood returns from the body to the right atrium, moves to the right ventricle, and is pumped to the lungs.
Oxygen-rich blood returns from the lungs to the left atrium, moves to the left ventricle, and is pumped out to the rest of the body.
Common heart-related conditions include arrhythmia, heart failure, and coronary artery disease.
